DR.A.P.J.ABDUL KALAM

Introduction

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as the Missile Man of India and the People’s President, was one of the most inspiring personalities of modern India. He was a scientist, a teacher, a visionary, and above all, a humble human being who dedicated his entire life to the service of the nation. His journey from a small town boy in Rameswaram to becoming the 11th President of India remains an inspiring story for millions.

Life History

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the Missile Man of India, was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Coming from a simple family, he supported his studies by selling newspapers as a boy. He completed his education in aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Dr. Kalam joined ISRO, where he was the project director of India’s first satellite launch vehicle SLV-3. Later, as part of DRDO, he played a key role in developing the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which earned him the title Missile Man of India. He also contributed to the success of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. In 2002, he became the 11th President of India and was lovingly called the People’s President. Though he faced failures, like being rejected as a fighter pilot and experiencing failed rocket launches, he never gave up. Instead, he saw failure as “First Attempt In Learning.” Dr. Kalam received many awards, including the Bharat Ratna. He wrote inspiring books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds. He passed away on 27 July 2015 while addressing students, leaving behind a legacy of knowledge, humility, and inspiration.
